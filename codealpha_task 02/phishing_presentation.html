What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers impersonate trusted entities to steal sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data.

  • Goal: Trick you into revealing confidential information
  • Method: Fake emails, websites, texts, or calls
  • Target: Personal data, login credentials, financial info
  • Impact: Identity theft, financial loss, data breaches

How to Spot Phishing Emails

  • Urgent language - "Act now!" or "Account will be closed!"
  • Generic greetings - "Dear Customer" instead of your name
  • Suspicious sender - Check email address carefully
  • Poor grammar/spelling - Professional companies proofread
  • Unexpected attachments - Don't open if not expected
  • Suspicious links - Hover to preview destination

Pro Tip: When in doubt, contact the company directly through their official website or phone number.

Identifying Fake Websites

  • Check the URL carefully - Look for misspellings (amazom.com vs amazon.com)
  • Look for HTTPS - Secure sites have a lock icon in the address bar
  • Examine the design - Poor quality graphics or layout
  • Verify contact information - Legitimate sites have clear contact details
  • Check for typos - Professional sites are well-written
  • Trust your instincts - If something feels off, it probably is

Never enter sensitive information on suspicious websites!

Social Engineering Tactics

Attackers use psychological manipulation to trick people:

  • Authority: Pretending to be your boss, IT department, or government
  • Urgency: Creating false deadlines to pressure quick action
  • Fear: Threatening account closure or legal action
  • Curiosity: "You've won a prize!" or "See who viewed your profile"
  • Trust: Impersonating friends, colleagues, or familiar brands
  • Helpfulness: Offering to "help" with a problem you didn't know you had

Remember: Legitimate organizations won't ask for sensitive information via email or phone.

Best Practices & Tips

  • Verify before you trust - Contact the sender through official channels
  • Use strong, unique passwords - Enable two-factor authentication
  • Keep software updated - Install security patches promptly
  • Think before you click - Hover over links to see where they lead
  • Report suspicious emails - Forward to your IT security team
  • Educate others - Share knowledge with family and colleagues
  • Trust your gut - If something seems fishy, it probably is!
